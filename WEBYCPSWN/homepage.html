Kalpana Chawla

Following cosmonaut Rakesh Sharma, who launched in a spacecraft in 1984, Kalpana Chawla was the first Indian-born woman and the second Indian to travel into space. Chawla's maiden mission saw her complete nearly 10.4 million miles in 252 orbits of the planet, clocking over 372 hours in space.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai, a Pakistani activist who was born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Swat Valley, spoke out in front of the public while still an adolescent against the Tehrik-e-Taliban Pakistan's ban on girls receiving an education (TTP; sometimes called Pakistani Taliban).

Mary Kom

Mary Kom, an Indian boxer from the Manipur state in northeast India, was born on March 1st, 1983, as Mangte Chungneijang. She also goes by the names Magnificent Mary and MC Mary Kom. Mary Kom is well-known for being a five-time world boxing champion and the only person to place at each of the six world championships.

Lata Mangeshkar

On September 28, 1929, Lata Mangeshkar was born in Indore. She went on to become the most well-known playback vocalist in Bollywood history. She recorded a variety of records and sang for actresses for more than 50 years, including Nargis and Preity Zinta (ghazals, pop, etc). She was often referred as as "India's Queen of Melody Nightingale."

What is women impowerment?

Women's empowerment may be defined in several ways, including accepting women's viewpoints or making an effort to seek them, raising the status of women through education, awareness, literacy, and training content.

What is the government doing to increase women empowerment?

Schemes that were introduced under the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme includes Ladli scheme, Kanyashree Prakalpa Yojana, Sukanya Samridhi Yojana, Balika Samridhi Yojana, Ladli Laxmi Yojana, Dhanalakshmi scheme and many more.

What is the role of women in local government?

They are serving as teachers, doctors, engineers, administrators and even heads of state. The need is to increase this ratio. More participation among the women means more progress of the society and is a measure of equality.

Women empowerment in india?

The principle of gender equality is enshrined in the Indian Constitution in its Preamble, Fundamental Rights, Fundamental Duties and Directive Principles. The Constitution not only grants equality to women, but also empowers the State to adopt measures of positive discrimination in favour of women
